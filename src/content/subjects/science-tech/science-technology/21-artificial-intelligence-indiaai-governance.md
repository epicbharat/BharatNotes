## Introduction

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation of human intelligence processes by computer systems — including learning, reasoning, perception, and decision-making. AI is rapidly reshaping every domain — healthcare, agriculture, governance, defence, finance, and education. For UPSC GS Paper III, AI is relevant across Science & Technology, Economy (productivity, jobs), and Governance (e-governance, ethics). India's ambitious **IndiaAI Mission** (2024) and the global regulation debate (EU AI Act vs India's soft-touch approach) are critical exam-ready topics.

---

## AI — Core Concepts

### Building Blocks

| Concept | Definition |
|---------|-----------|
| **Artificial Intelligence (AI)** | Broad field — machines performing tasks requiring human intelligence |
| **Machine Learning (ML)** | AI subset — systems learn from data without explicit programming |
| **Deep Learning** | ML subset — multi-layered neural networks mimicking the human brain; powers image recognition, NLP |
| **Neural Networks** | Interconnected nodes processing information in layers; inspired by biological neurons |
| **Natural Language Processing (NLP)** | AI processing and understanding human language (text, speech) |
| **Generative AI** | AI that creates new content (text, images, code, video) — based on Large Language Models (LLMs) |
| **Large Language Models (LLMs)** | Massive neural networks trained on huge text datasets; examples: GPT-4, Gemini, LLaMA |

### Key AI Applications

- **Healthcare:** Medical imaging analysis, drug discovery, clinical documentation, diagnostic support
- **Agriculture:** Crop disease detection, precision farming, soil analysis
- **Finance:** Fraud detection, credit scoring, algorithmic trading
- **Governance:** e-Governance automation, targeted scheme delivery, surveillance
- **Defence:** Autonomous vehicles, intelligence analysis, cyber warfare
- **Education:** Personalised learning, automated grading

## IndiaAI Mission

### Cabinet Approval

The **Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs (CCEA)** approved the **IndiaAI Mission** in **March 2024** with an outlay of **₹10,371.92 crore** (approximately ₹10,372 crore) over **five years** (FY 2024-25 to FY 2028-29).

**Nodal Ministry:** Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY)
**Implementing Agency:** IndiaAI — an Independent Business Division under Digital India Corporation (DIC)

### Seven Pillars of IndiaAI Mission

| Pillar | Budget Allocation | Key Objective |
|--------|------------------|---------------|
| **1. IndiaAI Compute Capacity** | ₹4,563.36 crore | Build AI computing infrastructure with **10,000+ GPUs** for startups, researchers, government |
| **2. IndiaAI Innovation Centre (IAIC)** | ₹1,971.37 crore | Foundational AI model research; develop Large Multimodal Models (LMMs) in Indian languages |
| **3. IndiaAI Datasets Platform** | ₹199.55 crore | Access to non-personal public sector datasets for AI research; DataCall process |
| **4. IndiaAI Application Development Initiative** | ₹689.05 crore | AI solutions for **11 priority sectors** (agriculture, health, education, smart cities, etc.) |
| **5. IndiaAI FutureSkills** | ₹882.94 crore | AI literacy, skilling, and upskilling; integration in 5,000 schools and 1,000 colleges |
| **6. IndiaAI Startup Financing** | ₹1,942.50 crore | Deep-tech AI startups through fund-of-funds, grants, soft loans |
| **7. Safe and Trusted AI** | Part of framework | Governance, ethics, indigenous regulatory tools, Responsible AI guidelines |

### Strategic Vision

- Position India as a **global AI hub**, not just a consumer of AI technologies
- Bridge the compute gap — India lacks large-scale GPU infrastructure compared to US/China
- Ensure AI is aligned with Indian needs (multilingual, diverse, socially inclusive)
- Create **homegrown AI models** in Indian languages (22 scheduled languages)

## National Data Governance Framework Policy (NDGFP)

India's **National Data Governance Framework Policy (NDGFP)** — published as a **draft in 2022** — focuses on **non-personal data** (aggregate, anonymised public sector data):

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Purpose** | Enable access to government/public datasets for AI training and research |
| **Mechanism** | India Datasets Platform; DataCall process for structured data requests |
| **Principle** | Privacy-preserving access; non-personal and anonymised data only |
| **Governance** | India Data Management Office (IDMO) under MeitY |
| **Linkage** | Complements Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 (which covers personal data) |

## AI Regulation — Global Models

### EU AI Act (2024)

The **EU Artificial Intelligence Act** — the world's first comprehensive binding AI law — entered into force on **1 August 2024** (to be fully applicable by **2 August 2026**, with exceptions):

| Risk Level | Examples | Obligations |
|------------|----------|-------------|
| **Unacceptable Risk (Prohibited)** | Social scoring by governments; biometric surveillance in real-time; subliminal manipulation; AI predicting criminal offences based on profiling | **Banned** from EU market (effective 2 February 2025) |
| **High Risk** | AI in critical infrastructure, education assessment, employment decisions, law enforcement, biometric identification | Strict: risk management, data quality, transparency, human oversight, accuracy requirements |
| **Limited Risk** | Chatbots, deepfakes | Transparency obligations — users must know they're interacting with AI |
| **Minimal Risk** | Spam filters, AI in video games | No mandatory requirements |

**Governance:** EU AI Office established within European Commission; GPAI-model obligations from August 2025.

### US Executive Order on AI (October 2023)

President Biden's Executive Order on "Safe, Secure, and Trustworthy AI" (October 2023):
- Required safety testing and sharing results with government for powerful AI models
- Directed federal agencies to assess AI risks
- Focused on AI's impact on national security, labour market, bias

**Note:** The Trump administration (January 2025) revoked this EO and issued a new one promoting US AI dominance without certain safety requirements.

### China's AI Regulations

- **Generative AI Regulation (2023):** Providers must register; content must not threaten national security or public morals; traceability requirements
- **Algorithm Recommendation Rules (2022):** Transparency in recommendation algorithms
- China's approach: State-led, focused on content control and national security

## India's AI Regulatory Approach

India has adopted a **"soft-touch" or innovation-first approach** — deliberately avoiding heavy-handed legislation:

| Feature | India's Approach |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Regulation** | Advisory and voluntary guidelines (not binding legislation) |
| **Philosophy** | "Responsible AI for All" — inclusive, human-centric AI development |
| **Framework** | NITI Aayog's Principles for Responsible AI (2021); MeitY advisory on AI |
| **IT Rules 2023** | Amendment added provisions on deepfakes — platforms must act within 24-36 hours on removal requests |
| **Rationale** | Avoid stifling innovation; India is still building AI capacity and cannot afford overregulation |
| **Criticism** | Lack of clear binding rules on algorithmic bias, high-risk AI in governance, consumer protection |

**MeitY AI Advisory (March 2024):** Directed large AI platforms to obtain government approval before deploying "under-tested" or "unreliable" AI models in India — this advisory was later softened after industry pushback.

## Algorithmic Bias and AI Ethics

### What is Algorithmic Bias?

Algorithmic bias occurs when an AI system produces **systematically prejudiced results** due to:
- **Biased training data** (historical discrimination reflected in data)
- **Flawed model design** (proxy variables correlating with protected attributes)
- **Feedback loops** (biased outputs reinforce biased inputs)

### Examples

- **Facial recognition:** Studies show significantly higher error rates for women and people with darker skin tones (NIST FRVT study)
- **Hiring algorithms:** Amazon's AI hiring tool (2018) penalised resumes from women
- **Criminal justice:** COMPAS recidivism algorithm in the US — higher false-positive risk scores for Black defendants
- **Credit scoring:** Algorithmic models may replicate historical redlining/loan discrimination

### India-Specific Concerns

- Aadhaar-based AI systems and exclusion of genuine beneficiaries
- AI in judicial or law enforcement applications — bias against marginalised communities
- Deepfakes targeting women, minorities, political opponents

### Principles for Trustworthy AI (OECD/NITI Aayog)

- **Fairness:** Non-discrimination, inclusive design
- **Accountability:** Clear responsibility for AI decisions
- **Transparency:** Explainability — users know how decisions are made
- **Safety and Robustness:** AI systems are reliable and secure
- **Privacy:** Data protection by design

## Deepfakes and Disinformation

**Deepfakes** are hyper-realistic AI-generated fake images, videos, or audio, typically created using GANs (Generative Adversarial Networks).

### Threats

- **Electoral interference:** Fake videos of political leaders; synthetic misinformation
- **Non-consensual intimate imagery (NCII):** Deepfake pornography — disproportionately targets women
- **Financial fraud:** Voice cloning for impersonation scams
- **National security:** Fabricated videos to incite communal tensions

### India's Response

- **IT (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules 2021, Amendment 2023:** Platforms must remove deepfake content within **24 hours** (for sexual content) and **72 hours** (for other content) of receiving a complaint
- **Consultation:** MeitY is working on comprehensive deepfake policy framework
- **Legislation gap:** No specific "deepfake law" yet; working within IT Act framework

## Lethal Autonomous Weapons Systems (LAWS)

**LAWS** — also called "killer robots" — are weapons that can identify and engage targets without meaningful human control.

| Position | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **India's position at UN (CCW)** | India has called for an **international regulatory framework** on LAWS within the Convention on Certain Conventional Weapons (CCW) at the UN; supports human control over use of force; has not signed any ban treaty |
| **Global debate** | Divide between states wanting a **ban** (Austria, New Zealand, Brazil) vs states wanting **voluntary guidelines** (US, Russia, India); no binding treaty yet |
| **Concerns** | Accountability gap (who is responsible for civilian harm?); arms race risk; violation of IHL (International Humanitarian Law) |

## AI and Jobs — Future of Work

The **WEF Future of Jobs Report 2025** key findings relevant for UPSC:
- AI will displace **85 million jobs** but create **97 million new roles** globally by 2025 (net positive, but transition challenges are massive)
- Jobs most at risk: Routine data entry, basic customer service, repetitive manufacturing, basic accounting
- New roles created: AI trainers, prompt engineers, data analysts, cybersecurity experts, AI ethicists
- India-specific concern: Large informal sector workers (domestic workers, construction, agriculture) less likely to benefit immediately; risk of **"job polarisation"**

## AI in Healthcare

- **NLP for clinical documentation:** Auto-generates clinical notes from doctor-patient conversations (AIIMS Delhi pilot)
- **Radiology AI:** AI-assisted detection of TB, cancer, diabetic retinopathy (Aravind Eye Care + Google AI collaboration)
- **Drug discovery:** AI shortens drug development from 10-15 years to 3-5 years (AlphaFold — protein folding prediction by DeepMind)
- **National Digital Health Mission (ABDM):** AI analytics on Health ID records for population health management
- **Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission:** AI for fraud detection in insurance claims

## Exam Strategy

**For Prelims:** Focus on — IndiaAI Mission approval (March 2024), total outlay (₹10,372 crore), number of GPUs (10,000+), 7 pillars of IndiaAI Mission, GPAI founding year (2020), India's GPAI chairmanship (2022), EU AI Act in force (1 August 2024), risk categories of EU AI Act, India's approach (soft-touch/innovation-first), deepfake removal deadline under IT Rules (24/72 hours).

**For Mains (GS Paper III):** Key themes:
1. "IndiaAI Mission — assess its potential to make India a global AI hub. What challenges remain?"
2. "Compare EU AI Act's risk-based approach with India's soft-touch regulation — which model is more appropriate for India?"
3. "Algorithmic bias poses serious risks to constitutional rights — examine with examples and suggest safeguards"
4. "AI and jobs — threat or opportunity for India's workforce? Suggest a transition strategy"
5. "Role of GPAI and multilateral cooperation in governing AI at global level"

**Key linkages:**
- AI + Privacy = Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 (GS II)
- AI + Defence = Lethal Autonomous Weapons (GS III)
- AI + Agriculture = Krishi AI, precision farming (GS III)
- AI + Jobs = Reskilling, New Education Policy (GS II + III)
