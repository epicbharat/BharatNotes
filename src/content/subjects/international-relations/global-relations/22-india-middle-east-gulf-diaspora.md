## Introduction

The Middle East — encompassing the Arab Gulf states (GCC), Israel, Iran, Iraq, Jordan, Egypt, and Yemen — represents one of India's most strategically vital regions. India's engagement with West Asia is driven by **four pillars**: energy security (~46% of crude oil imports), **diaspora remittances** (~$45 billion annually from GCC), **trade and investment**, and **regional security**. PM Modi articulated India's enhanced engagement through the "**Link West**" policy, recognising that economic convergence, not just historical ties, must drive relations.

## India-UAE Relations

### Comprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement (CEPA)

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Signed** | 18 February 2022 |
| **In force** | 1 May 2022 |
| **Nature** | Comprehensive Economic Partnership Agreement (CEPA) |
| **Trade target** | US$100 billion in bilateral non-oil trade (within 5 years) |
| **Services target** | US$15 billion in services trade |
| **Trade performance** | Grew from US$72.9 billion (2021-22) to US$84.5 billion (2022-23) — 16% increase |
| **Tariff concessions** | UAE gave duty-free access on 97% of India's tariff lines; India on ~80% |

### Key Areas of Cooperation

- **Energy:** Abu Dhabi National Oil Company (ADNOC) investments in India; UAE is among India's top crude oil suppliers; strategic petroleum reserve cooperation
- **Investments:** Abu Dhabi Investment Authority (ADIA) and Mubadala have made significant investments in Indian infrastructure and startups
- **Digital and fintech:** UPI linkage with UAE payment systems; rupee-dirham trade settlement
- **Defence:** Joint exercises; defence equipment procurement discussions
- **Diaspora:** ~4.3 million Indians in UAE — the largest Indian diaspora community in a single country

## India-Saudi Arabia Relations

### Strategic Partnership (2010)

India and Saudi Arabia elevated ties to a **Strategic Partnership** in 2010 during PM Manmohan Singh's visit. The relationship has deepened under PM Modi:

- **Delhi Declaration (2016):** PM Modi's visit — oil cooperation, counter-terrorism, investment
- **Riyadh Declaration (2019):** Saudi Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman's India visit; Saudi Arabia announced $100 billion investment plans in India (across refinery, infrastructure, technology)

### Key Pillars

| Pillar | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Oil imports** | Saudi Arabia supplies ~11-15% of India's crude oil imports (Iraq is the largest at ~20-24%) |
| **Saudi Vision 2030** | Aligns with India's "Make in India" — potential for investment in refining, chemicals, renewable energy |
| **ARAMCO-Reliance** | Saudi Aramco sought a 20% stake in Reliance Industries' O2C (Oil-to-Chemicals) business — deal under negotiation |
| **Counter-terrorism** | Cooperation on tracking terror financing; extradition of suspects |
| **Hajj** | Saudi Arabia accommodates ~1.75 lakh Indian Hajj pilgrims annually |
| **Diaspora** | ~2.65 million Indians in Saudi Arabia; largest source of Gulf remittances |

## India-Israel Relations

### History and Evolution

- Diplomatic relations established: **29 January 1992**
- India voted against Israel in early UN decades (reflecting NAM policy); shift post-1990s
- PM Modi made the **first-ever visit of an Indian PM to Israel in July 2017** — upgraded to "Strategic Partnership"

### Key Cooperation Areas

| Area | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Defence** | Israel is one of India's top 3 defence suppliers; UAV/drones (Heron series), radar systems, missile defence, Spike anti-tank missiles |
| **Agriculture** | Israeli drip irrigation technology (Netafim), protected cultivation, water management |
| **Technology** | Cybersecurity, high-tech startups, AI, R&D cooperation |
| **Counter-terrorism** | Intelligence sharing, joint training |

### October 7, 2023 Hamas Attack — India's Response

- Hamas launched a large-scale terrorist attack on Israel on **7 October 2023**, killing ~1,200 Israelis
- India issued a **strong condemnation of the terrorist attack**, expressing solidarity with Israel
- As the Gaza conflict escalated, India called for **restraint, protection of civilians, and humanitarian aid access**
- India voted **differently from previous years** in some UN General Assembly resolutions — reflecting a more calibrated balance between Israel partnership and traditional support for Palestinian cause
- India evacuated Indian nationals from Israel under **Operation Ajay (October 2023)**

## IMEC — India-Middle East-Europe Economic Corridor

### G20 2023 Announcement

The **India-Middle East-Europe Economic Corridor (IMEC)** was announced during the **G20 New Delhi Summit on 9 September 2023** — one of the summit's major deliverables.

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **MoU signed by** | India, USA, EU, France, Germany, Italy, UAE, Saudi Arabia |
| **Corridor route** | India → UAE (sea) → Saudi Arabia → Jordan → Israel → Greece/Italy/France (sea) |
| **Two wings** | East Corridor (India to Arabian Gulf); North Corridor (Gulf to Europe) |
| **Infrastructure** | Railway network, ports, road, electric cable, hydrogen pipeline, high-speed data cable |
| **Strategic purpose** | Connectivity alternative to China's **Belt and Road Initiative (BRI)** |
| **Challenge** | Israel-Gaza conflict (post-October 2023) has raised uncertainty about corridor's near-term viability |

## India-Iran Relations

### Chabahar Port

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Port** | Shahid Beheshti Port, Chabahar, southeastern Iran (on Arabian Sea/Indian Ocean) |
| **Agreement** | India signed a 10-year Chabahar Port development contract with Iran in **May 2024** (IPGL — India Ports Global Limited) |
| **Strategic value** | Only Iranian port with direct Indian Ocean access; enables India to bypass Pakistan to reach Afghanistan and Central Asia |
| **INSTC link** | Part of **International North-South Transport Corridor (INSTC)** — 7,200 km multi-modal route linking India to Russia/Europe via Iran |
| **INSTC route** | Mumbai → Chabahar (sea) → Tehran/Bandar Anzali (road) → Caspian Sea → Astrakhan → Russia/Europe |
| **Cost benefit** | FFFAI study: 30% cheaper and 40% shorter than traditional Suez Canal route |
| **US sanctions tension** | US sanctions on Iran periodically complicate Chabahar development; India has negotiated exemptions |

### INSTC Members

Original signatory members (2000): India, Iran, Russia. Now expanded to over 13 countries including Azerbaijan, Central Asian states, Turkey.

## Gulf Indian Diaspora and Remittances

### Scale of Indian Presence in GCC

| Country | Indian Population |
|---------|-----------------|
| UAE | ~4.3 million |
| Saudi Arabia | ~2.65 million |
| Kuwait | ~1.0 million |
| Qatar | ~830,000 |
| Oman | ~665,000 |
| Bahrain | ~350,000 |
| **Total GCC** | **~9.7 million** |

Total Indians abroad: **35.4 million** (world's largest diaspora). GCC accounts for ~27% of this.

### Remittances

| Year | India's Total Remittances | GCC Share |
|------|--------------------------|-----------|
| 2023-24 | US$118.7 billion (RBI survey) | ~38% |

**Key shift (2023-24):** Advanced economies (US, UK, Singapore, Canada, Australia) overtook GCC countries in remittance share for the first time, reflecting increasing migration of **skilled professionals** to developed countries vs **semi-skilled/unskilled workers** to Gulf.

- **Gulf remittances estimated:** ~$45 billion from GCC countries (~38% of $118.7 bn)
- Major source: UAE (19.2% of total), Saudi Arabia (6.7%), Qatar (4.1%)

## India's Energy Security — West Asia Dependence

### Crude Oil Imports (2023-24 data)

| Supplier | India's Import Share |
|----------|---------------------|
| Russia | ~36-41% (post-2022 Ukraine war, Russia became largest supplier) |
| Iraq | ~20-24% |
| Saudi Arabia | ~11-15% |
| UAE | ~9-11% |
| **GCC + West Asia total** | ~46% (down from 63% in 2017) |

**Key point:** While Russia has become the largest single supplier (discounted crude), West Asia collectively still accounts for ~46% of India's crude imports. The **Strait of Hormuz** — through which ~45-50% of India's crude oil passes — remains a critical chokepoint.

### India's Energy Strategy

- **IEA Membership:** India became a full member of the International Energy Agency (IEA) in 2021
- **Strategic Petroleum Reserves (SPR):** India has ~5-9 days of crude oil reserves in three locations (Vishakhapatnam, Mangaluru, Padur)
- **Diversification:** India is diversifying suppliers (Russia, US, Latin America) to reduce West Asian dependency
- **Renewables:** India's domestic renewable capacity growth (500 GW target by 2030) reduces long-term oil demand

## Abraham Accords (2020) — India's Perspective

The **Abraham Accords** (signed August-September 2020) normalised diplomatic relations between Israel and:
- **UAE** (August 2020)
- **Bahrain** (September 2020)
- **Sudan** and **Morocco** (October-December 2020)

**India's perspective:**
- India maintained studied neutrality during the accords
- The normalisation **benefited India** as it reduced the diplomatic tension between Israel and Gulf Arab states, allowing India to maintain close ties with both simultaneously
- The I2U2 grouping (formed 2021) was enabled in part by UAE-Israel normalisation
- IMEC corridor's viability also depends on Israel-Arab cooperation

## Yemen Conflict and India

- Yemen's civil war (since 2015) — Houthi rebels (Iran-backed) vs. Saudi-led coalition
- **India's evacuations:**
  - *Operation Raahat (April 2015)* — evacuated ~4,741 Indians and 960 foreign nationals from Yemen
- Post-October 2023: **Houthi attacks on Red Sea/Gulf of Aden shipping** — disrupted global trade; Indian-flagged or India-bound vessels targeted
- India's response: Deployed **INS warships** for anti-piracy and protective patrolling in the region; engaged with Houthis diplomatically to protect Indian nationals

## India's "Link West" Policy

PM Modi articulated the **"Link West"** policy as a complement to "Act East" — India must be as energetic in deepening Gulf/West Asia ties as it is in Southeast Asia:

- **Diplomatic visits:** PM Modi visited UAE (2015, 2019, 2023), Saudi Arabia (2016, 2019), Israel (2017), Iran (2016), Qatar (2023), Bahrain (2019)
- **"Look West" to "Link West":** Move beyond reactive engagement to proactive, multi-sector partnership
- **India-Arab League:** India participated in Arab League meetings as a dialogue partner
- **India-GCC FTA:** Negotiations for India-GCC Free Trade Agreement underway (stalled for years, revived 2023-24)
