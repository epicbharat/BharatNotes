## Formation and Evolution of the G20

The **Group of Twenty (G20)** was formally established on 26 September 1999 at the G7 Finance Ministers' meeting in Washington, D.C., with an inaugural ministerial meeting held on 15–16 December 1999 in Berlin. Its creation was a direct response to a series of financial crises in the 1990s — particularly the 1997–98 Asian Financial Crisis — which revealed that the G7 alone could not adequately manage the interconnected global economy.

**Elevation to Leaders' Summit (2008):** The G20 operated only at the Finance Ministers and Central Bank Governors level until the 2008 Global Financial Crisis triggered its elevation. The first G20 Leaders' Summit was held in Washington, D.C. on 14–15 November 2008, convened to coordinate a global response to the financial meltdown. This marked the G20's transformation from a technocratic forum to the premier platform for international economic cooperation.

## Composition

The G20 comprises **19 individual countries plus two regional blocs:**

Argentina, Australia, Brazil, Canada, China, France, Germany, India, Indonesia, Italy, Japan, Mexico, Russia, Saudi Arabia, South Africa, South Korea, Turkey, United Kingdom, United States of America + **European Union** (founding member) + **African Union** (admitted 2023).

**African Union (AU) Inclusion, September 2023:** At the New Delhi Summit on 9 September 2023, the African Union was formally admitted as the 21st permanent member of the G20 — the first expansion of the group since its founding in 1999. The AU represents 55 African nations and approximately 1.4 billion people. This was a major diplomatic achievement of India's Presidency and a recognition of Africa's growing economic weight.

## India's G20 Presidency 2023

India held the G20 Presidency from **1 December 2022 to 30 November 2023** — the first time India chaired the group.

**Theme:** "Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam — One Earth, One Family, One Future"

**Scale:** India hosted over 200 meetings and events across 60 cities, the largest multilateral exercise India has ever organised.

### New Delhi Declaration (9–10 September 2023)

The G20 Leaders' Summit in New Delhi adopted the **New Delhi Declaration** — a consensus document of 83 paragraphs (100% consensus across all 21 members, including on the Ukraine conflict language, which had been a sticking point). Key features:

- **African Union inclusion** formalised
- **Global Biofuels Alliance (GBA):** Launched by India on 9 September 2023 with founding members including the USA, Brazil, Argentina, and Italy; aims to accelerate sustainable biofuel adoption globally
- **India–Middle East–Europe Economic Corridor (IMEC):** MOU signed for a multimodal connectivity corridor linking India to Europe via the Middle East (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Jordan, Israel, Greece)
- **Debt restructuring:** Commitment to the Common Framework for debt restructuring; focus on debt distress in developing countries including Sri Lanka, Zambia, Ghana
- **Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI):** India's UPI, Aadhaar, DigiLocker experience promoted as a global model; G20 Framework for DPI adopted
- **LiFE (Lifestyle for Environment):** India's initiative on sustainable consumption promoted in the sustainable development chapter

### Voice of the Global South Summit

India convened the **Voice of the Global South Summit** — a parallel diplomatic initiative — in January 2023 (first edition) and November 2023 (second edition), bringing together over 100 developing nations to articulate a common position before and after the G20.

## Troika System

The G20 Presidency rotates among members according to a regional rotation system. The **Troika** consists of the current, immediate past, and incoming Presidencies working together to ensure continuity:

- 2022: Indonesia → 2023: India → 2024: Brazil (Troika for New Delhi Summit)
- 2025 Presidency: South Africa (first African nation to hold the G20 Presidency)

## G20 vs G7 vs BRICS — Comparison

| Feature | G20 | G7 | BRICS (since 2024) |
|---------|-----|----|--------------------|
| Founded | 1999 | 1975 | 2006 (BRIC) / 2010 (BRICS) |
| Members | 21 (19 + EU + AU) | 7 | 9 (Brazil, Russia, India, China, SA + Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran, UAE from Jan 2024) |
| Focus | Global economic governance, development | Advanced economy coordination | Emerging/developing economy coordination |
| Decision-making | Consensus; non-binding | Consensus; non-binding | Consensus; non-binding |
| Share of global GDP | ~85% | ~45% | ~35% |
| India's role | Member, 2023 Chair | Guest invitee | Founding member |

## Significance for India

The G20 Presidency gave India an unprecedented platform to:
1. Project soft power and demonstrate organisational capacity on a global stage
2. Advance an agenda centred on the Global South — debt relief, DPI, climate finance, food security
3. Shape multilateral discourse on digital governance and sustainable development
4. Elevate the African Union, building diplomatic capital across 55 African states
