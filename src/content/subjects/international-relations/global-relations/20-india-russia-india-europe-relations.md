India's relations with Russia and Europe represent two of the most consequential and evolving pillars of its foreign policy. India-Russia ties — built over seven decades on defence dependency, nuclear cooperation, and strategic convergence — are being tested by the Russia-Ukraine war and shifting geopolitical alignments. Simultaneously, India's engagement with Europe has deepened significantly, driven by trade agreements, technology partnerships, and shared concerns about China and global governance. Navigating both relationships simultaneously, while maintaining strategic autonomy, defines India's diplomatic tightrope in the 2020s.

For UPSC GS2, these relationships are tested through questions on defence procurement, energy security, India's neutral stance on Ukraine, the EU-India FTA, and the concept of multi-alignment.

## Part I: India-Russia Relations

### Historical Foundation

| Milestone | Year | Significance |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| Diplomatic relations established | 1947 | Soviet Union among the first to recognise independent India |
| **Indo-Soviet Treaty of Peace, Friendship and Cooperation** | 9 August 1971 | 20-year treaty; Article IX provided for mutual consultations in case of military threat; crucial during the 1971 Bangladesh Liberation War when the USSR vetoed anti-India resolutions at UNSC |
| Treaty renewed | 8 August 1991 | Extended for another 20 years |
| **Declaration on Strategic Partnership** | 2000 | Signed during President Putin's first visit to India |
| **Special and Privileged Strategic Partnership** | 2010 | Elevated during PM Manmohan Singh's visit to Moscow; India's highest-level partnership designation |
| Annual summits institutionalised | 2000 onwards | India-Russia Annual Summits, 2+2 ministerial dialogue (from 2021) |

> The 1971 Treaty was India's most significant deviation from formal non-alignment. It was prompted by the US-China-Pakistan strategic convergence and provided a critical security umbrella during the Bangladesh Liberation War. The USSR vetoed three UNSC resolutions calling for ceasefire, allowing India's military operations to conclude.

### Defence Cooperation

India-Russia defence ties represent the most extensive military-technical cooperation between any two countries outside a formal alliance structure.

#### Major Defence Platforms

| System | Type | Status |
|--------|------|--------|
| **S-400 Triumf** | Air defence missile system | 5 regiment deal signed 2018 ($5.43 billion); delivery of all 5 units completed by 2024 |
| **BrahMos** | Supersonic cruise missile | Joint venture: DRDO + NPO Mashinostroyenia; range 290 km (extended versions being tested); now exported to Philippines |
| **AK-203 rifles** | Assault rifle | Joint venture: Indo-Russian Rifles Private Ltd at Amethi, UP; production of 6.71 lakh rifles for Indian Army |
| **INS Vikramaditya** | Aircraft carrier | Ex-Gorshkov; commissioned 2013 |
| **Krivak-class frigates** | Guided missile frigates | 4 frigates (2 built in Russia, 2 in India at Goa Shipyard) |
| **Su-30MKI** | Multi-role fighter | 272 licensed-produced by HAL; backbone of IAF fleet |
| **T-90 tanks** | Main battle tank | Licensed production; ~1,000+ in Indian Army |

India sources approximately **60–65% of its defence equipment from Russia/Soviet-origin**, making it the most Russia-dependent major military in the world outside Russia's immediate neighbourhood.

#### S-400 and CAATSA

The **Countering America's Adversaries Through Sanctions Act (CAATSA)** — US legislation — imposes mandatory sanctions on countries making "significant" defence transactions with Russia, Iran, or North Korea (Section 231).

| Issue | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **CAATSA trigger** | India's S-400 deal ($5.43 billion, signed October 2018) is clearly a "significant" transaction |
| **US position** | US has repeatedly signalled it does not want to sanction India but has not formally granted a waiver |
| **Waiver provision** | CAATSA allows Presidential national security waiver; Congress has repeatedly questioned whether India deserves one |
| **India's argument** | Defence diversification takes time; India cannot immediately replace Russian platforms; sanctions would damage the strategic partnership |
| **Current status (2026)** | India has not been sanctioned despite S-400 deliveries; de facto tolerance by the US pending India's defence diversification |

### Russia-Ukraine War: India's Stance

Russia launched its full-scale invasion of Ukraine on **24 February 2022**. India's response:

#### India's Position

- Abstained on **UN General Assembly resolutions** condemning Russia (Resolution ES-11/1, March 2022 and subsequent resolutions)
- Consistently called for **dialogue and diplomacy** and respect for the UN Charter
- Has not condemned Russia's invasion directly
- Deepened oil imports from Russia (opportunistic use of discounted crude)
- Maintained bilateral ties with Russia while engaging diplomatically with Ukraine

#### India's Reasoning

| Factor | Explanation |
|--------|------------|
| **Defence dependency** | India cannot afford to alienate Russia while 60%+ of its military hardware is Russian-origin; spare parts, maintenance critical |
| **Nuclear cooperation** | Kudankulam NPP (Tamil Nadu) — built by Russian ROSATOM; two units operational, four more under construction |
| **Strategic autonomy doctrine** | India's multi-alignment policy resists being drawn into Western alliance politics |
| **China factor** | India does not want Russia to become entirely dependent on China — maintaining Russia as a counterbalance serves India's interests |
| **Historical relationship** | Deep political, institutional, and people-to-people ties spanning 75+ years |

#### Impact on Bilateral Trade

Russia-Ukraine war transformed India-Russia economic relations:

| Metric | Pre-war (FY 2021-22) | Post-war (FY 2024-25) | Change |
|--------|----------------------|----------------------|--------|
| Bilateral trade | ~$10 billion | ~$69 billion | 6x increase |
| Russian oil share in India's imports | <1% | ~36% (~87.4 MT) | Massive surge |
| Russia's rank as oil supplier | Not in top 5 | **India's largest oil supplier** | Structural shift |

India benefited enormously from discounted Russian crude (trading at $25–35/barrel discount to Brent immediately after sanctions). However, US sanctions imposed on Russian oil majors Rosneft and Lukoil in November 2025 complicated Indian refiners' operations, prompting some diversification.

#### Rupee-Ruble Trade

A significant portion of India-Russia trade shifted to **rupee-ruble settlement** (bypassing USD/SWIFT systems under US/EU sanctions). However, accumulated rupee balances in Russian accounts created challenges — Russia found it difficult to spend rupee surpluses since India has limited import demand for Russian non-oil goods.

### Challenges in India-Russia Relations

| Challenge | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Russia-China proximity** | Russia-China "no limits" partnership (Feb 2022) uncomfortable for India; China's strategic gains from Russia's isolation concern India |
| **Western pressure** | US, EU pressure India to reduce Russia engagement; affects technology transfer and defence co-production |
| **Rupee surplus problem** | Russia has accumulated billions in rupee-denominated accounts it cannot easily utilise |
| **Supply chain disruptions** | Sanctions have disrupted logistics, payment channels, and spare parts supply |
| **Defence diversification** | India is actively diversifying to French (Rafale), US (C-17, P-8I, MQ-9B), Israeli, and domestic (LCA Tejas) platforms — structural shift away from Russia over time |
| **INSTC delays** | International North-South Transport Corridor linking India-Iran-Russia has faced delays |

## Part II: India-Europe Relations

### India-EU Strategic Partnership

The **India-EU Strategic Partnership** was established in **2004** during the 5th India-EU Summit in The Hague.

### Key Institutional Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **India-EU Summit** | Annual/biennial leaders' meeting |
| **Trade and Technology Council (TTC)** | Launched 2023; facilitates cooperation on technology standards, supply chains, digital governance, clean energy |
| **India-EU Green Alliance** | Bilateral cooperation on climate, clean energy, sustainable finance |

### India-EU FTA: Long Road

| Phase | Period | Status |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Negotiations launched | 2007 | High Ministerial Council meeting |
| Negotiations suspended | 2013 | After 16 rounds; impasse on investment, IPR, GIs, government procurement |
| Negotiations relaunched | June 2022 | Renewed political will post-pandemic and post-Ukraine war |
| **FTA concluded** | **January 2026** | Historic conclusion after intense negotiations |

The EU-India FTA (concluded January 2026) covers goods tariffs, services, investment protection, geographical indications, government procurement, and sustainability provisions. It is expected to significantly boost bilateral trade (currently ~€115 billion/year) and provide India with a secure, high-standards market for manufactured goods, pharmaceuticals, and IT services.

### India-Germany: Green and Sustainable Development Partnership

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Partnership signed** | 2022 (Inter-Governmental Consultations, Berlin) |
| **Focus areas** | Clean energy transition, green hydrogen, EV ecosystem, sustainable finance |
| **German investment** | Germany committed substantial investment in India's green energy sector |
| **Strategic dialogue** | India-Germany Strategic Partnership (2001); deepened through 6th IGC (July 2022) |

Germany is India's largest trading partner in Europe. Bilateral trade ~€30 billion/year.

### India-France: Strategic Partnership

France was India's **first European strategic partner** (1998 Strategic Partnership). The relationship has deepened significantly through defence, space, and nuclear cooperation.

| Milestone | Year | Significance |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| Strategic Partnership | 1998 | First European strategic partnership for India |
| **Rafale deal** | 2016 | 36 Rafale aircraft; first batch delivered 2020; strengthened by additional naval Rafale order (26 aircraft for Indian Navy, 2023) |
| Space cooperation | Ongoing | ISRO-CNES cooperation; France supported India's space programme |
| Macron as Republic Day Chief Guest | January 2024 | Macron visited India 25–26 January 2024 as Chief Guest at Republic Day |
| **India-France Joint Statement (January 2024)** | January 2024 | Comprehensive joint statement covering defence, space, civil nuclear, digital economy, climate, education |

The January 2024 Macron visit and joint statement marked a significant deepening of the partnership, with the two sides calling it a **"trusted, reliable, and long-term" strategic partnership**. Areas covered included the Horizon 2047 roadmap (a 25-year bilateral vision document).

> Note: The frontmatter references a "Jaisalmer Declaration 2024" — this likely refers to bilateral defence/energy meetings in Jaisalmer, but the principal high-level event in 2024 was the Republic Day visit and accompanying Joint Statement. Always cite the verified January 2024 India-France Joint Statement.

### India-UK: Comprehensive Strategic Partnership

India and the UK have a **Comprehensive Strategic Partnership** (2021), deepened by PM Modi's visit to UK for the G7 and bilateral talks.

#### India-UK Comprehensive Economic and Trade Agreement (CETA)

| Timeline | Event |
|----------|-------|
| January 2022 | FTA negotiations formally launched |
| Multiple rounds | 14+ rounds of negotiations over 3 years |
| **6 May 2025** | FTA finalised |
| **24 July 2025** | CETA signed |

#### Key Features of India-UK CETA

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Tariff elimination** | India to eliminate tariffs on 99% of UK tariff lines (100% of trade value) |
| | UK to reduce tariffs on 90% of Indian tariff lines |
| **Whisky and gin** | UK Scotch whisky tariffs to be phased down from 150% to much lower levels over several years |
| **Automobiles** | UK auto (EV) tariffs reduced; India maintains regulatory standards |
| **Services** | Mode 4 movement of professionals; India wins mobility concessions for IT/business services workers |
| **IPR** | Balanced provisions; India successfully defended data protection and pharmaceutical patent positions |
| **Projected benefit** | UK: ~£4.8 billion annual economic boost by 2040 |

### India and NATO: No Formal Alliance, Growing Coordination

India is **not a NATO member** and has consistently maintained that joining formal alliances is incompatible with its strategic autonomy doctrine. However, there is growing functional cooperation.

| Area | Status |
|------|--------|
| **Interoperability** | India's growing exercises with NATO members (India-US, India-France, India-UK joint drills) |
| **Technology sharing** | India-US ICET (Initiative on Critical and Emerging Technologies); India-UK-Australia defence tech |
| **Indo-Pacific coordination** | NATO's Indo-Pacific "partners" framework — India participates in select NATO events/platforms |
| **Ukraine war** | India's abstentions strained relations; NATO countries want India to take clearer pro-Ukraine positions |
| **No Article 5 participation** | India firmly resists any collective defence commitments |

India's engagement with NATO is best characterised as **issue-specific, non-committal coordination** rather than alliance-building.

### Nordic Countries and India

India's relations with Nordic countries (Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Finland, Iceland) have strengthened around **clean energy, innovation, and green technology**.

| Country | Key Area of Cooperation |
|---------|------------------------|
| **Sweden** | Defence (Gripen consideration; SAAB collaboration); innovation; trade |
| **Norway** | Seafood; maritime technology; sovereign wealth fund investment in India |
| **Denmark** | Green shipping; offshore wind energy; food processing |
| **Finland** | Telecom (Nokia 5G); education; design |

India-Nordic Summits (first held 2018, Stockholm) provide a multilateral platform. Nordic countries are significant investors in Indian green energy and fintech.

## India's Strategic Autonomy: Multi-Alignment Doctrine

India's approach to great power relations is governed by the doctrine of **strategic autonomy** — the ability to pursue an independent foreign policy that serves India's national interest without being locked into any bloc or alliance.

### Key Tenets

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|------------|
| **Multi-alignment** | Engage with the US, Russia, EU, China, Global South simultaneously; maintain leverage with each |
| **Conditional engagement** | Cooperate on specific issues; refuse alliance commitments |
| **Issue-by-issue approach** | India may vote with the US on climate, abstain on Ukraine, cooperate with Russia on defence, compete with China economically |
| **Development primacy** | Economic development and national security take precedence over ideological solidarity |

### India's Multi-Alignment in Practice (2025-26)

| Relationship | Cooperation | Tension |
|-------------|------------|---------|
| **USA** | Quad, defence, technology (ICET), trade | CAATSA, H-1B visas, tariff disputes |
| **Russia** | Oil imports, defence, INSTC | Western pressure, rupee surplus |
| **EU** | FTA (concluded 2026), TTC, green alliance | Data localisation, auto tariffs |
| **China** | Bilateral trade (~$113 billion imports from China) | Border disputes, RCEP exit, competitive anxiety |
| **Middle East** | I2U2, energy, diaspora | Iran sanctions complications |

## Exam Strategy

**For Prelims:**
- 1971 Treaty: Signed 9 August 1971 — 20-year Treaty of Peace, Friendship and Cooperation
- Special and Privileged Strategic Partnership: elevated in 2010
- S-400: Signed October 2018, $5.43 billion; deliveries completed ~2024
- CAATSA: 2017 US law; India has not been formally sanctioned; no formal waiver granted
- BrahMos: India's DRDO + Russia's NPO Mashinostroyenia; supersonic; exported to Philippines
- AK-203: Joint venture at Amethi, UP
- Russia as India's oil source: ~36% share in FY 2024-25; ~87.4 million tonnes
- India-UAE CEPA: in force May 2022
- India-UK CETA: signed July 2025
- India-EU FTA: concluded January 2026
- India-EU Strategic Partnership: established 2004

**For Mains:**
- Russia relations: Organise as historical foundation → defence cooperation (S-400, BrahMos, AK-203) → energy (oil surge post-2022) → Ukraine war stance → challenges (Russia-China proximity, CAATSA) → future trajectory
- India's Ukraine abstention: Give both sides — strategic calculation vs normative critique; conclude with India's national interest framing
- EU-India FTA: Contextualise within the 2007-2013 failure, 2022 relaunch, 2026 conclusion; cover goods, services, GIs; link to strategic goal of trade diversification
- India-UK CETA: Key features — whisky tariff, services mobility, 90/99% tariff coverage
- Strategic autonomy: Not the same as non-alignment; multi-alignment involves simultaneous engagement with competing powers; India's weight in global politics allows this
- India-France: Rafale (36 jets + 26 naval), space, Horizon 2047 roadmap
- Nordic countries: Clean energy, innovation — connects to India's green transition narrative
