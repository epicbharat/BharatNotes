## What is the Lithium Triangle?

The **Lithium Triangle** is a region in the **Andes Mountains of South America** spanning parts of **Argentina, Bolivia, and Chile** that contains more than **half of the world's known lithium reserves**. The region holds an estimated **50 million metric tons** of identified lithium resources across vast **salt flats (salars)** -- the Salar de Uyuni in Bolivia (the world's largest salt flat), the Salar de Atacama in Chile, and the salars in northwestern Argentina's Jujuy, Salta, and Catamarca provinces.

Lithium is a critical mineral for the **global energy transition**, as it is the key raw material for **lithium-ion batteries** used in electric vehicles (EVs), energy storage systems, consumer electronics, and grid-scale storage. Of the world's **86 million tons of identified lithium resources**, Bolivia possesses approximately **21 million tons**, Argentina **19.3 million tons**, and Chile **9.6 million tons**. The extraction method in these regions is primarily **brine-based** -- lithium-rich brine is pumped from underground aquifers into large evaporation ponds where solar energy concentrates the lithium over 12--18 months.

The three countries have adopted **different approaches** to lithium governance: **Chile** operates through public-private partnerships with strong state oversight (SQM and Albemarle are key producers); **Argentina** has a more liberalized, market-friendly mining regime attracting global investment; and **Bolivia** has pursued state-led development with a 51% government stake in joint ventures, including a **$1 billion deal with a Chinese-led consortium (CATL/CBC)** signed in late 2024 for Direct Lithium Extraction (DLE) plants at Uyuni.

## Current Status / Latest Data

- **Argentina** forecasts a **75% production increase** to 130,800 tons of lithium carbonate equivalent (LCE) in 2025 and aims to become the **second-largest global producer by 2026** (up to 500,000 tons by 2030).
- **Chile**: In April 2025, President Boric mandated all new lithium contracts to operate as **public-private partnerships** with a significant state role.
- **Bolivia** signed a **$1 billion agreement (late 2024)** with a Chinese-led consortium (CBC/CATL) for DLE plants at Uyuni; the state retains a **51% stake**.
- Global lithium demand is projected to grow **5x by 2030** driven by EV adoption, making the Lithium Triangle increasingly geopolitically significant.
- **India** discovered lithium reserves in **Jammu & Kashmir's Reasi district** (estimated 5.9 million tons) in February 2023, potentially reducing import dependence.

## UPSC Exam Corner

### Prelims: Key Facts
- Lithium Triangle: **Argentina, Bolivia, Chile** (South America)
- Contains over **50% of the world's lithium reserves**
- Bolivia has the **largest identified lithium resources** (~21 MT)
- **Salar de Uyuni** (Bolivia) = world's largest salt flat and lithium source
- Extraction method: **brine evaporation** (traditional) and **DLE** (emerging)
- India's lithium discovery: **Reasi, J&K** (February 2023)

### Mains: Probable Themes
1. **Resource nationalism** in the Lithium Triangle -- differing governance models and their effectiveness
2. **Geopolitics of critical minerals** -- competition between the US, China, and EU for lithium supply chains
3. **Environmental impact** of lithium extraction -- water depletion in arid regions, indigenous community rights
4. India's strategy for **critical mineral security** -- J&K discovery, Khanij Bidesh India Ltd (KABIL), mineral partnerships
5. **Energy transition** and the growing importance of lithium for achieving net-zero targets
