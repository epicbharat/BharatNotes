## What is the Digital Divide?

The digital divide refers to the **gap between those who have access to modern information and communication technologies (ICTs)** — including the internet, computers, and smartphones — **and those who do not**. In India, this divide manifests across multiple axes: **rural vs urban, rich vs poor, male vs female, and young vs old**. It represents one of the most significant barriers to equitable development in the 21st century.

India had approximately **1.03 billion internet users** at the end of 2025, with an overall internet penetration of about **70%**. However, this national average masks stark disparities. Urban internet penetration reaches as high as **88% in Tier-1 cities**, while rural penetration stands at approximately **37%**. Twice as many rural households (**16.7%**) lack internet access compared to urban households (**8.4%**). Only **3.8% of rural households** have high-speed fibre connections, compared to **15.3% in urban areas**.

Critically, the digital divide is not just about infrastructure. A major study found that **nearly half of rural and over 40% of urban offline households** remain disconnected not because of lack of access, but because they **do not know what the internet is or how to use it** — highlighting the centrality of **digital literacy** as a barrier.

The digital divide has far-reaching implications for **equity in governance, education, healthcare, and economic opportunity**. As India rapidly digitises public services — from Aadhaar-based authentication to UPI payments, from DigiLocker to CoWIN — those without digital access or literacy are effectively **excluded from the benefits of digital governance**. The COVID-19 pandemic starkly exposed this divide when schools shifted online, with only **24% of Indian households** having internet access for education (NSSO). The divide is also **deeply gendered**: patriarchal norms in many communities restrict women's access to smartphones, creating a digital gender gap that compounds existing socio-economic inequalities.

## Current Status / Latest Data

- **BharatNet project** aims to connect all 6.4 lakh villages with optical fibre; as of 2025, Phase III is underway but implementation remains behind the original schedule, with cost escalations and right-of-way issues.
- **Digital India Programme** has expanded digital infrastructure, but quality of connectivity (broadband vs 2G/3G) remains a concern in rural and remote areas.
- India's **mobile data costs** are among the lowest globally (~Rs 10-15 per GB), yet affordability of smartphones (even entry-level at Rs 5,000-7,000) remains a barrier for the poorest households.
- **PM-WANI** (Wi-Fi Access Network Interface) scheme aims to create public Wi-Fi hotspots through Public Data Office Aggregators and small entrepreneurs.
- The **National Digital Literacy Mission (NDLM)** and **PMGDISHA** have trained over 6 crore citizens, but gaps persist in functional digital literacy — knowing how to use government portals, digital payments, and online services.
- **COVID-19 exposed the digital divide starkly**: during school closures, only 24% of Indian households had internet access for online education (NSSO), with rural children worst affected.
- **UPI transactions** crossed 14 billion per month in 2025, but adoption remains concentrated in urban and semi-urban areas; digital payments penetration in rural India is still limited.
- The **gender digital divide** is acute: men are approximately 36% more likely to use the internet than women, with the gap widest in rural areas and among older age groups.

## UPSC Exam Corner

### Prelims: Key Facts
- India had ~1.03 billion internet users in 2025 (70% penetration)
- BharatNet targets optical fibre connectivity to all 6.4 lakh villages
- PM-WANI provides public Wi-Fi hotspots via Public Data Offices (PDOs)
- PMGDISHA = Pradhan Mantri Gramin Digital Saksharta Abhiyan (targets one person per rural household)
- Rural internet penetration ~37% vs urban ~88% in Tier-1 cities
- Only 3.8% of rural households have fibre connections vs 15.3% urban
- India ranks among the cheapest mobile data markets globally

### Mains: Probable Themes
1. "The digital divide in India is as much about literacy as it is about infrastructure." Discuss
2. Analyse the multidimensional nature of the digital divide in India — rural-urban, gender, age, and income
3. Evaluate government initiatives like BharatNet and Digital India in bridging the digital divide
4. How does the digital divide exacerbate existing social and economic inequalities in India?
