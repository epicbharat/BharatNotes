## What is the Multidimensional Poverty Index (MPI)?

The **Multidimensional Poverty Index (MPI)** is a measure of **acute poverty that goes beyond income** to capture deprivations across **three dimensions: health, education, and standard of living**, using **10 indicators**. It was developed by the **United Nations Development Programme (UNDP)** and the **Oxford Poverty and Human Development Initiative (OPHI)** in 2010. A person is identified as multidimensionally poor if they are deprived in **at least one-third** (33.33%) of the weighted indicators.

The **Global MPI** is published annually by UNDP-OPHI, while India has its own **National MPI** developed by **NITI Aayog** in collaboration with UNDP (first published in 2021). India's National MPI uses **12 indicators** across the same three dimensions, aligned with government schemes such as Swachh Bharat, PM Ujjwala Yojana, Jal Jeevan Mission, and PM Awas Yojana, and draws data from the **National Family Health Survey (NFHS)**.

India has achieved a **historic reduction** in multidimensional poverty -- from **55.1% in 2005-06** to **16.4% in 2019-21** (Global MPI), and from **29.17% in 2013-14** to **11.28% in 2022-23** (NITI Aayog). Approximately **248.2 million people** escaped multidimensional poverty between 2013-14 and 2022-23.

## Key Features

| # | Feature | Details |
|---|---------|---------|
| 1 | **Developed by** | UNDP and OPHI (Global MPI); NITI Aayog (India's National MPI) |
| 2 | **Dimensions** | Health, Education, Standard of Living |
| 3 | **Global MPI Indicators** | 10 indicators (nutrition, child mortality, schooling, attendance, cooking fuel, sanitation, water, electricity, housing, assets) |
| 4 | **India National MPI** | 12 indicators (adds bank account and maternal health) |
| 5 | **Poverty Threshold** | Deprived in at least 1/3 of weighted indicators |
| 6 | **Data Source (India)** | NFHS (National Family Health Survey) |
| 7 | **Methodology** | Alkire-Foster method (AF method) |
| 8 | **MPI Value** | Product of headcount ratio (H) and intensity of poverty (A): MPI = H x A |

## Current Status / Latest Data

- **Global MPI 2025:** India's MPI value is **0.069**, reflecting steady improvement. **1.1 billion people** (18.3%) across 109 countries live in acute multidimensional poverty globally.
- **NITI Aayog (2024 report):** **248.2 million people** escaped multidimensional poverty between 2013-14 and 2022-23; headcount ratio dropped from **29.17% to 11.28%**.
- **State-level milestone:** **Kerala** became the first state declared **free from extreme poverty** (1 November 2025).
- **Rural-urban divide:** Rural poverty declined faster; rural areas accounted for bulk of poverty reduction.
- **Worst performing indicators:** Nutrition and years of schooling remain the most deprived indicators.
- India is no longer the country with the highest number of multidimensionally poor people (overtaken by Nigeria in recent UNDP reports).

## UPSC Exam Corner

### Prelims: Key Facts
- MPI measures poverty across 3 dimensions and 10 indicators (Global MPI)
- Developed by UNDP and OPHI; India's National MPI by NITI Aayog
- A person is MPI-poor if deprived in at least 1/3 of weighted indicators
- India's MPI headcount fell from 55.1% (2005-06) to 16.4% (2019-21) per Global MPI
- Kerala is the first state declared free from extreme poverty (November 2025)
- Uses Alkire-Foster methodology

### Mains: Probable Themes
1. How does the Multidimensional Poverty Index provide a more comprehensive picture of poverty than income-based measures? Discuss with reference to India
2. Evaluate India's progress in reducing multidimensional poverty and the role of government schemes in this achievement
3. Despite declining MPI, India continues to face challenges in nutrition and education indicators. Analyse
4. Compare the Global MPI and India's National MPI -- their methodological differences and policy relevance
