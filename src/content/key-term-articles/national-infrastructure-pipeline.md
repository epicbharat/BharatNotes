## What is the National Infrastructure Pipeline (NIP)?

The **National Infrastructure Pipeline (NIP)** is a comprehensive **project database and investment roadmap** launched by the Government of India in **December 2019** to provide world-class infrastructure and improve the **quality of life** for citizens. It was announced by **Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman** in the Union Budget 2019-20. A **Task Force** headed by the **Economic Affairs Secretary** was constituted to identify and aggregate infrastructure projects across sectors.

The NIP was originally envisioned to cover the period **FY2020 to FY2025** with an estimated investment of **Rs 111 lakh crore** (~$1.5 trillion). The projects are funded through a combination of the **Centre (39%)**, **states (39%)**, and **private sector (22%)**. The NIP is hosted on the **India Investment Grid (IIG)** portal, which serves as a one-stop digital platform for investors to discover infrastructure investment opportunities.

The NIP covers **33 sectors** (expanded from 25 originally) spanning transport (roads, railways, airports, ports), energy, urban infrastructure, water, digital infrastructure, social infrastructure (health, education), and industrial infrastructure. It is closely integrated with the **PM Gati Shakti National Master Plan** for multi-modal connectivity planning.

## Current Status / Latest Data

- **Projects (March 2025):** **13,000 projects** with total estimated cost of **Rs 185 lakh crore** (~$2.1 trillion), nearly doubled from the initial Rs 111 lakh crore.
- **Sector concentration:** Nearly **half** of the investment is in the transport sector (roads, railways, airports, ports).
- **Recent additions:** ~3,500 projects worth Rs 25 lakh crore added in FY2025 alone, with Rs 17-18 lakh crore in transport (roads and bridges).
- **Sectors expanded:** From 25 sectors to **33 sectors** and from 57 sub-sectors to **79 sub-sectors** by March 2025.
- **Project completion:** Only **20%** of projects completed as of March 2024; work underway on another 45%.
- **Private sector share:** Remains limited at less than **1%** of NIP projects; PPP share approximately **11%**.
- **Capital expenditure (Budget 2025-26):** Rs **11.21 lakh crore** (3.1% of GDP); Railways CAPEX at record Rs 2,65,200 crore.
- **NaBFID:** National Bank for Financing Infrastructure and Development (established 2021) is the key development finance institution supporting NIP projects.

## UPSC Exam Corner

### Prelims: Key Facts
- NIP was launched in December 2019; covers 33 sectors and 13,000+ projects
- Estimated investment: Rs 185 lakh crore (as of March 2025)
- Funding: Centre (39%), States (39%), Private (22%)
- Task Force headed by Economic Affairs Secretary
- Hosted on India Investment Grid (IIG) portal
- NaBFID is the dedicated DFI for infrastructure financing

### Mains: Probable Themes
1. Evaluate the role of the National Infrastructure Pipeline in addressing India's infrastructure deficit and attracting private investment
2. Despite the NIP's ambitious targets, private sector participation remains minimal. Analyse the challenges and suggest measures to enhance PPP in infrastructure
3. Discuss the linkage between NIP and PM Gati Shakti in improving multi-modal connectivity and reducing logistics costs in India
4. Critically examine the financing challenges of the NIP and the role of innovative instruments like InvITs, municipal bonds, and DFIs
