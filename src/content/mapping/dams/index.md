Dams are tested in UPSC both as geography (river-dam mapping, states, reservoir names) and as current affairs (inter-state water disputes, dam safety, irrigation projects). Key distinctions to remember: Tehri is India's tallest dam; Hirakud is the longest; Indira Sagar has the largest reservoir by volume. The Narmada, Krishna, and Cauvery river systems have the most disputed dams.

## Major Dams of India

| Salal Dam | Chenab | J&K | Hydro (690 MW) | — | First major project on Chenab in J&K |

| Sardar Sarovar Dam | Narmada | Gujarat | Irrigation, Hydro, Drinking water | Sardar Sarovar Reservoir | 163 m tall; largest dam in Gujarat; Narmada Bachao Andolan (NBA) protests; benefits Gujarat, MP, Rajasthan, Maharashtra |

| Mettur Dam | Kaveri | Tamil Nadu | Irrigation, Hydro | Stanley Reservoir | Oldest dam in TN (1934); supplies water to TN's delta; opening date (June 12) monitored annually for monsoon |
